Four years ago, I started at an education technology company as employee number five. Since then, I’ve gone from not knowing what “user experience design” meant to playing a pivotal role in product strategy, leading design projects, and ultimately creating an experience that fundamentally changes (and improves!) the way teachers receive professional development.

I’ve fallen in love with the mix of analytical and creative thinking that being a product designer offers, and come to understand that the profession is first and foremost about asking the right questions. This happens to be pretty convenient for someone who has never missed an opportunity to ask ask why * in her life.

I’m passionate about solving the problems (and making sure they are the right ones to solve), and I l love participating in the entire end-to-end process of design: from need-finding to idea generation, intuitive user flows to clear and beautiful visual pixels, and finally working closely with engineering partners on the all important execution details. I’m deeply concerned with the well-being of my colleagues and bringing joy to the workplace is important to me. Sometimes this means I'm just a little bit mischievous — I've been known to play an office prank (or two). In my free time, I love to run, enjoy San Francisco's beautiful weather, and dream about owning a Bernese Mountain Dog (or two).
